Methodology for seismic hazard characterization, synthetic earthquake generation and design spectra for the moon.

Creating safe habitats in space is difficult due to the lack of atmosphere, oxygen, stable temperature and threats such as meteorite impacts and radiation. Advanced technologies are being developed to address these challenges, such as resilient structures and oxygen delivery systems, among others. Research is key to facilitating space exploration and colonization. One challenge is to develop lunar seismic hazard spectra (Figure 1) To obtain seismic hazard spectra on the Moon, data collected by lunar missions, such as the seismometers installed on the Apollo missions, have been used. These data help to better understand lunar seismic activity and to model seismic hazard spectra for future missions and construction projects on the Moon.
