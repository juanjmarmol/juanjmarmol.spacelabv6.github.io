Preliminary approach to assess the seismic hazard on the Moon.

The interest and desire of humanity to build extraterrestrial habitats brings with it great scientific and engineering challenges. To guarantee the safety and resilience of these habitats, it is necessary to characterize the hazards to which they will be exposed. This paper presents an overview to assess the seismic hazard at a specific site on the Moon, using a probabilistic seismic hazard model (PSHA).

Thesis

Preliminary assessment of seismic hazard at a lunar site.

During the Apollo missions on the Moon, a seismic network was deployed that recorded more than 12,500 seismic events in eight years. Among them, 28 were shallow moonquakes up to magnitude 4.1. These earthquakes represent a risk for future lunar bases. This thesis proposes a preliminary assessment of the seismic hazard at a lunar site imposed by shallow moonquakes. The assessment is performed using the classical probabilistic methodology, with the inclusion of a logic tree, in order to consider the epistemic uncertainties of the natural phenomenon.

Development of an application for the analysis of lunar seismic records.

The lunar seismic network was installed by the Apollo program since 1969, allowing to detect moonquakes of different types, of which it was evidenced that some moonquakes reached magnitudes of 5 on the Richter scale. This work proposes the development of a software called SignalMoon for the processing of lunar seismic signals and visibility of seismic parameters.
