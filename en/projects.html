Projects

Methodology for seismic hazard characterization, synthetic earthquake generation and design spectra for the moon

The need for the design of sustainable human extraterrestrial settlement structures is leading to the characterization of the different types of hazards to which extraterrestrial buildings will be exposed.

Performance design of a lunar habitat subjected to seismic loading

A structure built on the lunar surface will be affected by multiple natural hazards due to the lack of atmospheric protection and the highly hostile environment, including moonquakes, meteorite impacts, radiation and extreme temperature changes.

Performance design of a lunar habitat subjected to seismic loads and meteorite impacts.

The design of safe and sustainable extraterrestrial human settlements represents a major scientific and technological challenge for humanity. Future permanent habitats on the Moon and Mars may facilitate space exploration by serving as transit points for manned missions to other planets.
